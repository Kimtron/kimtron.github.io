Colons

Rule Example
A colon usually introduces a list or quoted speech.

There were three people present: the magistrate, the accused and the prosecutor.

The judge said: 'Not guilty.'

It can also be used to amplify or create contrast. There is only one word for it: cruel.
Use a colon to introduce a direct question when the question amplifies or modifies the introductory word or phrase. The question is this: who does the buck stop with?
A colon is used to introduce subtitles of books and articles in periodicals. The Neighbourhood Justice Centre: Problem solving approaches to justice

Semicolons

If you are unsure how to use a semicolon, don't use one.

They are often misused in dot-point lists to separate each point - this is what the dot-point is for.

Rule Example
You may use a semicolon if one or more items in a series or list within a sentence have internal commas. The report draws on a number of sources, including current thinking in sentencing; risk analysis, which has reached prominence in therapeutic justice; and the socio-economic studies.
